Harry Poter and the Philosopher's Stone

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone is the first book in the Harry Potter series written by J.K. Rowling. It introduces readers to the magical world of wizards and follows the journey of a young boy named Harry Potter, who discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard.

Discription

Chapter 1

The Boy Who Lived

  • Introduction of the Dursleys:

    • The chapter begins by introducing Vernon and Petunia Dursley, their mundane life, and their aversion to anything out of the ordinary.

  • The Mysterious Cat:

    • A tabby cat, later revealed to be Professor McGonagall in disguise, is seen reading a map and observing the Dursleys, foreshadowing the magical world's involvement.

  • The Arrival of Harry:

    • Albus Dumbledore and Rubeus Hagrid bring baby Harry to the Dursleys, leaving him with a letter explaining the situation.

  • The Death of the Potters:

    • The chapter reveals that Lord Voldemort killed Harry's parents, James and Lily Potter, but mysteriously failed to kill Harry, leaving him with a lightning bolt scar.

    Chapter 2

    The Vanishig Glass

    • Harry's Life with the Dursleys:

      • The chapter opens by highlighting the harsh reality of Harry's life with the Dursleys, living in a cupboard under the stairs and constantly tormented by his cousin Dudley.

    • The Zoo Trip:

      • The Dursleys take Harry and Dudley to the zoo for Dudley's birthday. This outing provides the setting for the magical incident with the snake.

    • The Vanishing Glass:

      • While looking at a boa constrictor, Harry begins talking to the snake. When Dudley pushes Harry, the glass of the snake's enclosure disappears, and the snake escapes.
